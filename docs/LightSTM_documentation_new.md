# pyLightSTM: Simulation of High-Resolution Molecular Imaging with Tip-Enhanced Electro-Luminescence

## Introduction

LightSTM is an advanced scanning probe-microscopy technique that combines Scanning Tunneling Microscopy (STM) with optical spectroscopy to achieve high-resolution imaging of molecules. The method leverages the optical cavity formed by the metallic STM tip, providing both spatial and spectroscopic information about molecular systems. Simulating the measured signal therefore requires modelling both processes: (1) electron tunneling that populates excited states and (2) coupling of molecular fluorescence into the optical cavity.

## Theoretical Background

### 1. STM Tunneling Signal

The tunneling current modulates the luminescence by controlling the population of excited states. Within the implementation the complex tunneling amplitude $T(R_{\text{tip}})$ is assembled on a Cartesian canvas by stamping molecular orbitals and convolving them with the tip wavefunction through `makeSTMmap`. The measurable tunneling signal is the squared magnitude of this amplitude,

$$ t_{\mathrm{STM}}(R_{\text{tip}}) = |T(R_{\text{tip}})|^2, $$

with

$$ T(R_{\text{tip}}) = \int \psi_{\text{tip}}(r-R_{\text{tip}})\,\psi_{\text{mol}}(r)\,\mathrm{d}r. $$

Here $\psi_{\text{tip}}$ is the tip wavefunction generated by `makeTipField` in STM mode (typically $\psi_{\text{tip}}(r) \propto e^{-\beta |r|}$) and $\psi_{\text{mol}}$ is the molecular orbital represented on the shared canvas. The convolutions are evaluated via FFTs so that complex phases from orbital coefficients are retained before taking the magnitude squared.

### 2. Optical Cavity Coupling

Optical coupling between molecular transitions and the tip cavity is encoded in the complex amplitude $O(R_{\text{tip}})$ constructed by `makePhotonMap`:

$$ O(R_{\text{tip}}) = \int \rho_{\text{trans}}(r)\,\phi_{\text{tip}}(r-R_{\text{tip}})\,\mathrm{d}r, $$

which yields the photon intensity

$$ I_{\text{opt}}(R_{\text{tip}}) = |O(R_{\text{tip}})|^2. $$

The transition density $\rho_{\text{trans}}$ is stamped onto the shared canvas, while the tip field $\phi_{\text{tip}}$ is generated through a multipole expansion in `makeTipField`:

$$ \phi_{\text{tip}}(r) = \sum_{\ell m} c_{\ell m}\, Y_{\ell m}(\theta, \phi)\, \frac{1}{\sqrt{r^2 + \sigma^2}}, $$

where $Y_{\ell m}$ are the real spherical harmonics implemented in `fieldFFT.getSphericalHarmonic`. Adjusting the multipole coefficients (for example adding $p_z$ or $d_{z^2}$ components) directly modifies the symmetry and nodal structure of the resulting photon maps.

### 3. FFT-Based Acceleration

Both optical and tunneling amplitudes are evaluated efficiently via FFT-based convolutions:

$$ M(R_{\text{tip}}) = \int f_{\text{sample}}(r)\, g_{\text{tip}}(r-R_{\text{tip}})\,\mathrm{d}r = \mathcal{F}^{-1}\!\left[\mathcal{F}(f_{\text{sample}})\, \mathcal{F}(g_{\text{tip}})\right]. $$

On the discretised canvas this becomes the discrete convolution

$$ M(R_{\text{tip}}) \approx \sum_{\mathbf{r}} f_{\text{sample}}(\mathbf{r})\, g_{\text{tip}}(\mathbf{r}-R_{\text{tip}})\,\Delta V, $$

with periodic wrap handled by the FFT grid shifts in `convFFT`. For quasi-3D photon maps only the in-plane $(x,y)$ directions undergo FFT; the $z$-layers are processed slice by slice to prevent artificial periodicity along the stamping axis while keeping the computation tractable.

### 4. Signal Combination and Image Formation

Photon emission requires both efficient excitation (tunneling) and efficient radiation (optical coupling). The code evaluates these contributions independently:

1. **STM Signal Generation** (`makeSTMmap`, `makeSTMmap_2D`):
   - Stamps molecular orbitals onto the canvas and convolves them with the STM decay kernel.
   - Produces complex amplitudes whose squared magnitudes give $I_{\text{STM}} = |T|^2$.

2. **Optical Signal Generation** (`makePhotonMap`):
   - Stamps transition densities and convolves them with the optical tip field, yielding $I_{\text{opt}} = |O|^2$.

3. **Combined Signal**:
   - Experimental photon yields depend on both $I_{\text{STM}}$ and $I_{\text{opt}}$. Post-processing typically multiplies or weights these maps according to the chosen kinetic model.
   - Additional modules (exciton solvers, charging models, diffusion networks) can modulate either channel before the final combination.

## Implementation Details

### Core Components

1. **Tip Field Generation** (`makeTipField` in `photo.py`)
   - Builds either optical multipole fields or STM decay kernels depending on the `bSTM` flag.
   - Supports 2D and 3D sampling; multipole dictionaries such as `{'s':1.0, 'pz':0.2}` weight the `fieldFFT.getSphericalHarmonic` basis and control field symmetry.

2. **Exciton System Solver** (`solveExcitonSystem` in `photo.py`)
   - Assembles Coulomb-coupled exciton Hamiltonians using the brute-force grid integral `GridUtils.coulombGrid_brute`:
     $$ J_{ij} = \int\!\int \frac{\rho_i(r)\,\rho_j(r')}{|r-r'|}\,\mathrm{d}r\,\mathrm{d}r' $$
     converted to electron-volts by the factor $14.3996\,\text{eV·Å}/e^2$.
   - Diagonalizes the Hamiltonian to obtain delocalized exciton energies and eigenvectors.

3. **Photon Map Generation** (`makePhotonMap` in `photonMap.py`)
   - Focuses on optical coupling: stamps transition densities, performs per-layer FFT convolutions, and stores $|O|^2$.
   - Works alongside `makeSTMmap`; both results are combined later during analysis or visualization.

### Key Functions and Dependencies

1. **Grid Operations** (`GridUtils.py`)
   - `makeTransformMat`: Builds lattice-to-canvas transforms by scaling lattice vectors with reciprocal grid counts and applying rotations.
   - `stampToGrid2D/3D`: Deposits densities or orbitals onto the shared canvas with bilinear/trilinear interpolation and optional complex coefficients.
   - `evalMultipole`: Interfaces with the C++ backend to compute dipole and quadrupole moments of stamped densities.

2. **FFT Operations** (`fieldFFT.py`)
   - Provides FFT helpers, spherical-harmonic basis functions, and grid shifting utilities used by both STM and optical workflows.

3. **Photon Mapping** (`photo.py`)
   - `photonMap2D_stamp`, `photonMap3D_stamp`: Stamp densities and execute FFT-based convolutions (2D FFT per slice for quasi-3D setups).
   - `convFFT`: Performs full 2D FFT convolution when the grid is treated uniformly.
   - `prepareRhoTransForCoumpling`: Optionally downsamples transition densities before exciton coupling.

### Workflow

1. **Initialization**
   - Load molecular geometries, wavefunctions, and transition densities.
   - Set up the simulation canvas, lattice vectors, and tip parameters (height, decay, multipole weights).
   - Optionally normalise or down-sample volumetric data prior to stamping.

2. **Exciton System Solution**
   - Prepare transition densities (`prepareRhoTransForCoumpling`), assemble the Hamiltonian, and solve for eigenvalues/eigenvectors.
   - Extract excitonic coefficients for constructing optical superpositions.

3. **Image Generation**
   - Compute tunneling amplitudes ($I_{\text{STM}}$) with `makeSTMmap`.
   - Compute optical amplitudes ($I_{\text{opt}}$) with `makePhotonMap`.
   - Combine, normalise, or analyse the resulting maps according to the experimental protocol.

## Function Reference

### Grid and Coordinate Operations (`photo.py` / `GridUtils.py`)

1. `makeTransformMat(ns, lvec, angle=0.0, rot=None)`
   - Creates transformation matrices by scaling lattice vectors with reciprocal grid counts and applying rotations; used during stamping and exciton coupling.

2. `getMGrid2D(nDim, dd)`, `getMGrid3D(nDim, dd)`
   - Generate FFT-centred coordinate grids and return axis shifts so the zero-frequency component aligns with FFT conventions.

3. `makeTipField(sh, dd, z0=10.0, sigma=1.0, multipole_dict={'s':1.0}, b3D=False, bSTM=False, beta=1.0)`
   - Constructs optical fields or STM decay kernels, optionally returning full 3D arrays for quasi-3D stamping.

4. `stampToGrid2D/3D(canvas, stamp, ...)`
   - Deposits orbitals or densities using bilinear/trilinear interpolation with optional complex coefficients for coherent superpositions.

### Exciton System Solver (`photo.py`)

1. `solveExcitonSystem(rhoTranss, lvecs, poss, rots, nSub=None, byCenter=False, Ediags=1.0)`
   - Main coupled-exciton solver; down-samples densities if requested, assembles the Hamiltonian, and returns eigenvalues, eigenvectors, and the Hamiltonian matrix.

2. `assembleExcitonHamiltonian(rhos, poss, latMats, Ediags, byCenter=False)`
   - Computes direct Coulomb integrals with positional offsets and diagonal site energies.

3. `prepareRhoTransForCoumpling(rhoTrans, nsub=None, lvec=None)`
   - Down-samples dense transition densities while approximately preserving norms; accelerates Hamiltonian construction.

4. `GridUtils.evalMultipole(rho, rot=None)`
   - Evaluates multipole moments of stamped densities via the C++ backend.

### Photon Mapping (`photonMap.py` / `photo.py`)

1. `makeSTMmap(S, coefs, wfTip, dd_canv, byCenter=False)`
   - Produces tunneling maps $|T|^2$ from molecular orbitals and STM kernels.

2. `makePhotonMap(S, ipl, coefs, Vtip, dd_canv, byCenter=False, bDebugXsf=False)`
   - Generates photon intensity maps $|O|^2$ and optionally exports intermediate canvases for inspection.

3. `photonMap2D_stamp`, `photonMap3D_stamp`
   - Low-level convolution routines using full 2D FFT or per-slice FFT for quasi-3D systems.

4. `convFFT(F1, F2, bNormalize=False)`
   - Utility performing full-dimensional FFT-based convolution with optional normalisation.

### Additional Utilities

1. `GridUtils.coulombGrid_brute(rho1, rho2, pos1, pos2, lat1, lat2)`
   - Brute-force double integral for Coulomb coupling between discrete charge distributions.

2. `photo.makeTipField(..., bSTM=True)`
   - Generates pure exponential tunneling kernels with decay length $\lambda = 1/\beta$.

3. `photo.prepareRhoTransForCoumpling(..., nsub)`
   - Balances accuracy and performance; subsampling factors of 4–6 typically preserve coupling energies within $10^{-3}$ relative accuracy.

4. `GridUtils.saveXSF`, `GridUtils.loadCUBE`
   - Import/export volumetric data with consistent scaling to physical units.

## Additional Implementation Notes

### Canvas Stamping and FFT Strategy
- Orbitals and transition densities are normalised on their native grids and stamped using `GridUtils.stampToGrid2D/3D` with optional `byCenter` alignment and complex coefficients for coherent superpositions.
- For quasi-3D photon maps the convolution is evaluated per $z$-slice via 2D FFTs and accumulated, avoiding artificial periodicity along $z$ while retaining efficiency.
- Coordinate grids produced by `getMGrid*` are rolled so that FFT wrap corresponds to the periodic boundary conditions required by NumPy.

### Tip Field Symmetry and Physical Insight
- Multipole coefficients in `multipole_dict` weight spherical harmonic components; dominant `pz` terms strengthen out-of-plane fields, while adding `dxz` or `dyz` introduces nodal lines in the photon map.
- In STM mode the decay constant $\beta$ sets the effective tunneling length $\lambda = 1/\beta$ (typically 0.8–1.2 \AA$^{-1}$). Adjusting $\beta$ alters the lateral decay of $I_{\text{STM}}$.

### Coulomb Coupling Prefactor
- Coulomb integrals are computed in Cartesian coordinates using the brute-force kernel $1/|r-r'|$. The factor $14.3996\,\text{eV·\AA}$ converts the result from elementary charge units to electron-volts:
  $$ J_{ij} = 14.3996 \times \int\!\int \frac{\rho_i(r)\,\rho_j(r')}{|r-r'|}\,\mathrm{d}r\,\mathrm{d}r'. $$
- Diagonal terms can include on-site energies or self-interaction corrections supplied through `Ediags`.

### Optional Flags and Debug Aids
- `byCenter=True` recenters stamping so molecular centroids align with the supplied coordinates.
- `bComplex=True` enables complex coefficients when combining orbitals or transition densities (essential for coherent exciton superpositions).
- `params['volumetric']` toggles between 2D projected densities and full quasi-3D treatment in `makePhotonMap`.
- `bDebugXsf=True` writes intermediate canvases via `GridUtils.saveXSF` for external visualisation.

### Normalisation Considerations
- Cube files (`GridUtils.loadCUBE`) are scaled either to electron density (transition densities) or Hartree potentials; additional normalisation (e.g. `photo.normalizeGridWf`) may be required before quantitative comparisons.
- When down-sampling with `prepareRhoTransForCoumpling`, monitor the $L^2$ norms to ensure quantitative convergence—subsampling factors of 4–6 typically preserve energy couplings within $10^{-3}$ relative accuracy.
