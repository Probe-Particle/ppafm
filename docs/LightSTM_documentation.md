# pyLightSTM: Simulation of High-Resolution Molecular Imaging with Tip-Enhanced Electro-Luminescence

## Introduction

LightSTM is an advanced scanning probe-microscopy technique that combines Scanning Tunneling Microscopy (STM) with optical spectroscopy to achieve high-resolution imaging of molecules. The method leverages the enhancement of optical signals through the cavity formed by the metallic STM tip, providing both spatial and spectroscopic information about molecular systems. Simulation of the signal therefore comprise simpulation of both these processes (1) tunneling rate and (2) coupling of molecular fluorescence to optical cavity.

## Theoretical Background

### 1. STM Tunneling Signal

The tunneling current modulates the luminescence by controlling the population of excited states. Within the implementation, the complex tunneling amplitude $T(R_{\text{tip}})$ is assembled on a Cartesian canvas by stamping molecular orbitals and convolving them with the tip wavefunction (`makeSTMmap`). The resulting tunneling rate is

$$ t_{\mathrm{STM}}(R_{\text{tip}}) = |T(R_{\text{tip}})|^2 $$

with

$$ T(R_{\text{tip}}) = \int \psi_{\text{tip}}(r-R_{\text{tip}})\,\psi_{\text{mol}}(r)\,\mathrm{d}r, $$

where:
- $\psi_{\text{tip}}$ is the tip wavefunction (approximated by an exponential $\psi_{\text{tip}}(r) \propto e^{-\beta |r|}$ in the STM mode of `makeTipField`),
- $\psi_{\text{mol}}$ is the molecular orbital wavefunction represented on the canvas.

In practice, the convolution is evaluated in Fourier space, and the canvas stores both the complex amplitude and any phase information arising from molecular coefficients before the magnitude is squared to yield the tunneling current map.

### 2. Optical Cavity Coupling

The optical coupling between molecular transitions and the tip cavity is described by the interaction between the transition density of the molecule and the electromagnetic field of the tip. The complex optical amplitude $O(R_{\text{tip}})$, evaluated in `makePhotonMap`, is

$$ O(R_{\text{tip}}) = \int \rho_{\text{trans}}(r)\,\phi_{\text{tip}}(r-R_{\text{tip}})\,\mathrm{d}r, $$

with the measurable photon intensity obtained as

$$ I_{\text{opt}}(R_{\text{tip}}) = |O(R_{\text{tip}})|^2. $$

Here:
- $\rho_{\text{trans}}$ is the transition density between initial and final molecular states (stamped onto the shared canvas),
- $\phi_{\text{tip}}$ is the tip's cavity field generated by `makeTipField` using a multipole expansion,
- $R_{\text{tip}}$ is the lateral/vertical tip position,
- $r$ is the integration variable.

The tip field is modeled as

$$ \phi_{\text{tip}}(r) = \sum_{\ell m} c_{\ell m}\, Y_{\ell m}(\theta, \phi)\, \frac{1}{\sqrt{r^2 + \sigma^2}}, $$

where $Y_{\ell m}$ are real Cartesian forms of spherical harmonics implemented in `fieldFFT.getSphericalHarmonic`. The coefficients $c_{\ell m}$ encode the symmetry and strength of the cavity mode; higher-order multipoles (e.g. $p_z$, $d_{z^2}$) strongly affect the angular emission pattern captured in the photon maps.

### 3. FFT-Based Acceleration

Both the optical coupling and tunneling integrals are efficiently computed using Fast Fourier Transform (FFT), as they can be expressed as convolutions:

$$ M(R_{\text{tip}}) = \int f_{\text{sample}}(r)\, g_{\text{tip}}(r-R_{\text{tip}})\,\mathrm{d}r = \mathcal{F}^{-1}\!\left[\mathcal{F}(f_{\text{sample}}) \cdot \mathcal{F}(g_{\text{tip}})\right]. $$

On the discretized canvas this integral becomes

$$ M(R_{\text{tip}}) \approx \sum_{\mathbf{r}} f_{\text{sample}}(\mathbf{r})\, g_{\text{tip}}(\mathbf{r}-R_{\text{tip}})\, \Delta V, $$

with periodic wrapping handled implicitly by the FFT shifts in `convFFT`. For the quasi-3D photon workflow, only the in-plane $(x,y)$ directions are transformed, while the $z$ layers are processed slice-by-slice to avoid artificial periodicity along the stamping axis.

### 4. Signal Combination and Image Formation

The final LightSTM signal is formed by combining the optical coupling and tunneling contributions that are computed independently:

1. **STM Signal Generation** (`makeSTMmap`, `makeSTMmap_2D`):
   - Stamps molecular orbitals/wavefunctions on the canvas and convolves them with the STM tip orbital or decay kernel.
   - Returns complex tunneling amplitudes whose magnitude squared yields the current map $I_{\text{STM}} = |T|^2$.

2. **Optical Signal Generation** (`makePhotonMap`):
   - Stamps transition densities and convolves them with the optical tip field.
   - Produces photon intensity maps $I_{\text{opt}} = |O|^2$ that encode radiative decay probability and mode matching.

3. **Combined Signal**:
   - Experimental photon yields depend on both excitation (STM) and emission (optical) efficiencies. Post-processing typically multiplies or otherwise combines $I_{\text{STM}}$ and $I_{\text{opt}}$ depending on the assumed kinetics.
   - Additional factors (local charging, exciton funnels) can be layered on top using the excitonic solvers described below.

## Implementation Details

### Core Components

1. **Tip Field Generation** (`makeTipField` in `photo.py`)
   - Constructs either an optical multipole field or an STM decay kernel.
   - Supports both 2D and 3D sampling; the $b_{\text{STM}}$ flag switches between $e^{-\beta r}$ tunneling envelopes and $1/\sqrt{r^2+\sigma^2}$ optical amplitudes.
   - Multipole dictionaries (`{'s':1.0, 'pz':0.2, ...}`) weight the `fieldFFT.getSphericalHarmonic` basis and control field symmetry.

2. **Exciton System Solver** (`solveExcitonSystem` in `photo.py`)
   - Implements Coulombically coupled exciton calculations via `assembleExcitonHamiltonian`.
   - Uses the brute-force grid integral `GridUtils.coulombGrid_brute` to evaluate the interaction
     $$ J_{ij} = \int\!\int \frac{\rho_i(r)\,\rho_j(r')}{|r-r'|}\,\mathrm{d}r\,\mathrm{d}r', $$
     scaled by $14.3996\,\text{eV·Å}/e^2$.
   - Diagonalizes the Hamiltonian to obtain delocalized exciton energies and coefficients.

3. **Photon Map Generation** (`makePhotonMap` in `photonMap.py`)
   - Focuses on optical coupling: stamps transition densities, applies per-layer FFT-based convolutions, and stores $|O|^2$.
   - Works alongside `makeSTMmap` (for excitation) and does not directly mix the two signals—combination is deferred to analysis scripts.

### Key Functions and Dependencies

1. **Grid Operations** (`GridUtils.py`)
   - `makeTransformMat`: Builds the lattice-to-canvas transform by dividing lattice vectors by grid counts and applying molecular rotations.
   - `stampToGrid2D/3D`: Deposits wavefunctions or densities onto the shared canvas with bilinear/trilinear interpolation.
   - `evalMultipole`: Delegates to the C++ backend to compute dipole and quadrupole moments of stamped densities, enabling multipole analysis.

2. **FFT Operations** (`fieldFFT.py`)
   - Implements efficient Fourier transforms for convolution calculations
   - Manages periodic boundary conditions and grid alignments

3. **Photon Mapping** (`photo.py`)
   - `photonMap2D_stamp`, `photonMap3D_stamp`: Stamp densities and perform FFT-based convolutions (2D FFT per slice for the quasi-3D case).
   - `convFFT`: Performs full-dimensional FFT-based convolutions for purely 2D cases.
   - `prepareRhoTransForCoumpling`: Optionally downsamples and normalizes transition densities prior to exciton coupling.

### Workflow

1. **Initialization**
   - Load molecular geometries and wavefunctions
   - Set up computational grid and tip parameters
   - Initialize transition densities

2. **Exciton System Solution**
   - Assemble Hamiltonian matrix
   - Solve for eigenstates and energies
   - Compute coupling coefficients

3. **Image Generation**
   - Calculate tip fields and tunneling probabilities, producing $I_{\text{STM}}$.
   - Perform FFT-based convolutions for optical amplitudes, producing $I_{\text{opt}}$.
   - Combine the two maps (and, if needed, excitonic populations) during analysis to predict LightSTM photon yields.

## Function Reference

### Grid and Coordinate Operations (`photo.py`)

1. `makeTransformMat(ns, lvec, angle=0.0, rot=None)`
   - Creates transformation matrices for molecular orientations
   - Parameters: grid dimensions (`ns`), lattice vectors (`lvec`), rotation angle
   - Returns: transformation matrix for coordinate mapping

2. `getMGrid2D(nDim, dd)`, `getMGrid3D(nDim, dd)`
   - Generate coordinate grids for calculations with consistent FFT centering.
   - Parameters: dimensions (`nDim`), grid spacing (`dd`)
   - Returns: coordinate arrays (X, Y, Z) and grid shifts so that FFT wrap corresponds to periodic boundary conditions.

3. `makeTipField(sh, dd, z0=10.0, sigma=1.0, multipole_dict={'s':1.0}, b3D=False, bSTM=False, beta=1.0)`
   - Constructs tip field using multipole expansion
   - Supports both STM and optical field calculations
   - Parameters: shape (`sh`), spacing (`dd`), tip height (`z0`), decay parameters

### Exciton System Solver (`photo.py`)

1. `solveExcitonSystem(rhoTranss, lvecs, poss, rots, nSub=None, byCenter=False, Ediags=1.0)`
   - Main solver for coupled exciton systems.
   - Optionally downsamples densities, computes multipole moments, assembles the Hamiltonian, and returns $(E, V, H)$ tuples.

2. `assembleExcitonHamiltonian(rhos, poss, latMats, Ediags, byCenter=False)`
   - Constructs the Hamiltonian using direct grid integration of Coulomb interactions.
   - Incorporates positional shifts (via `byCenter`) and lattice transforms (`makeTransformMat`).

3. `prepareRhoTransForCoumpling(rhoTrans, nsub=None, lvec=None)`
   - Down-samples dense grids, preserving normalization where possible, and enables faster Hamiltonian assembly.
   - Used internally by `solveExcitonSystem`

### Photon Mapping (`photonMap.py`)

1. `makePhotonMap(S, ipl, coefs, Vtip, dd_canv, byCenter=False, bDebugXsf=False)`
   - Generates optical coupling maps
   - Supports both 2D and 3D calculations
   - Parameters: system (`S`), eigenvector index (`ipl`), coefficients, tip potential

2. `makeSTMmap(S, coefs, wfTip, dd_canv, byCenter=False)`
   - Calculates tunneling probability maps
   - Combines molecular and tip wavefunctions
   - Returns intensity map of tunneling current

### Utility Functions

1. `loadCubeFiles(S0)`
   - Loads molecular orbital data from Gaussian cube files
   - Handles both wavefunctions and transition densities

2. `loadDicts(fname, convertor=float)`
   - Loads parameter dictionaries from configuration files
   - Used for tip and molecular specifications

3. `plotPhotonMap(system, ipl, ncomb, nvs, byCenter=False, fname=None, dd=None)`
   - Visualizes calculated photon maps
   - Supports various output formats and plotting options

## Additional Implementation Notes

### Canvas Stamping and FFT Strategy
- Molecular orbitals and transition densities are normalised on their native grids and stamped onto the canvas using `GridUtils.stampToGrid2D/3D` with optional `byCenter` alignment and complex coefficients (for phase-sensitive superpositions).
- For quasi-3D photon maps the code iterates over $z$-layers, performing a 2D FFT on each slice and summing the resulting convolutions. This avoids spurious periodicity along $z$ while keeping the convolution cost manageable.
- FFT centering is handled by rolling the coordinate grids (`getMGrid*`) so that the zero-frequency component sits at the array origin required by NumPy FFT conventions.

### Tip Field Symmetry and Physical Insight
- The multipole coefficients in `multipole_dict` weight different spherical harmonic components. For example, a dominant `pz` term enhances out-of-plane fields, while adding `dxz` breaks cylindrical symmetry and yields nodal patterns in the photon map.
- In STM mode the `beta` parameter controls the decay length $\lambda = 1/\beta$, typically chosen to mimic vacuum tunneling (0.8–1.2 Å$^{-1}$). Adjusting $\beta$ directly rescales the lateral spread of the tunneling current.

### Coulomb Coupling Prefactor
- Coulomb integrals are computed in Cartesian coordinates using the brute-force kernel $1/|r-r'|$. The factor $14.3996\,\text{eV·Å}$ converts the result from elementary charge units to electron-volts:
  $$ J_{ij} = 14.3996 \times \int\!\int \frac{\rho_i(r)\,\rho_j(r')}{|r-r'|}\,\mathrm{d}r\,\mathrm{d}r'. $$
- Diagonal terms can include on-site energies or self-interaction corrections supplied through `Ediags`.

### Optional Flags and Debug Aids
- `byCenter=True` recenters stamping so that molecular centroids align with the requested coordinates.
- `bComplex=True` enables complex coefficients when combining orbitals or transition densities (essential for coherent exciton superpositions).
- `params['volumetric']` toggles between 2D projected densities and full quasi-3D treatment in `makePhotonMap`.
- Debug exports (`bDebugXsf`) write intermediate canvases via `GridUtils.saveXSF` for external visualisation.

### Normalisation Considerations
- Cube files (`GridUtils.loadCUBE`) are scaled either to electron density (transition densities) or Hartree potentials; subsequent normalisation (e.g. `normalizeGridWf`) may be required before physical comparisons.
- When down-sampling with `prepareRhoTransForCoumpling`, monitor the $L^2$ norms to ensure quantitative convergence—subsampling factors of 4–6 typically preserve energy couplings within $10^{-3}$ relative accuracy.
